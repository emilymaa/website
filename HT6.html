OVERVIEW

PROJECT

Hack the 6ix is the largest summer student run, not for profit hackathon located in the heart of Toronto. Every year, we select 400+ participants from a pool of over 1500 remarkable developers, designers, and innovators.

MY CONTRIBUTION

As a marketing director, I developed an integrated marketing plan by creating social media campaigns, email marketing, blogs, and forming cross organizational partnerships.

01
OUR CHALLENGE

For the first time ever, our team had to transition from our previously in-person event format to a fully virtual event.


At the same time, the pandemic has sparked many thriving new e-commerce

“How might we attract participants despite the new virtual format and provide the same value proposition while engaging with students?”

02
OUR STRATEGY

We wanted to leverage our social media channels and the hackathon community to create content that resonated with students and encouraged students to want to learn more about our event.



HUMANS OF HACK THE 6IX

We asked our team to share their own experiences with attending hackathons and the impact it had on them. Espicially with the new virtual environment, we wanted to bridge the disconnect through personal stories. We hoped to reach our audience on a more intimate level, and attract individuals of all backgrounds.


We focused on three main aspects:

1.Most memorable experience

2. Coolest project

3. First hackathon experience








GIVEAWAY

We held our first ever giveaway to incentivize students to participate and increase our social media presence.


Through this campaign we were able to reach over 100 students and boost our social media engagement by 30%!








HACKATHON KNOW-ALLS

With the goal of attracting a diverse set of partcipants ranging from any skill level, we created a set of posts to educate students on topics such as what a hackathon is, benefits to attending a hackathon, how to make the most of a hackathon experience, and digital hackathon essentials!







DIGITAL HACKATHON MUST HAVES

We wanted to explore all online mediums which is why we wrote our first ever blog post on digital hackathon must haves in hopes to prepare participants for the event.
