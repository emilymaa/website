overview

CHOMP is an interactive database to help you properly and easily dispose of your waste through scanning the product barcode. Our discovery page allows users to post questions, insights, and articles, therefore becoming educated on the importance of recycling while using the app.

our challenge:

Every year approximately 300 million tonnes of plastic waste is produced with close to 80% of these plastics accumulating in the natural environment and oceans. Only 9% of the waste generated each year in Canada is recycled.

our solution:

We created a platform to boast an innovative and comprehensive approach to reducing incorrect waste disposal by combining aspects of community, education, and encouragement.



user research:


After conducting a few surveys with students, we found the three main reasons preventing them from correctly disposing their waste.


Uneducated

People did not know how to correctly recycle.

Lazy

People were too lazy to correctly recycle.

Don't Care

People didn't know the impact of their actions.

our approach:

Educate.

The app aims to simplify recycling through an easy-to-use barcode scanner that directs the user on how to correctly dispose of waste.

Encourage.

Through an online community of users sharing their own tips and tricks through blog posts, they will be able to feel encouraged and connected to others with the same goal.

Impact.

With a monthly and yearly tracker that records how much waste is collected during that period, users can reflect and make improvements through suggested articles and see their personal impact.
